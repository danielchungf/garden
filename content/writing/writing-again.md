I used to write a lot when I was younger. In high school, I was the insufferable kid who would always correct his friends' grammar and word choice. I aced all my journalism classes while majoring in Communications in college, even though it was clear to me I wasn't going to pursue that career path.

I remember finding joy in writing. In articulating complex ideas in long documents, looking for the perfect words for every sentence. Ten years later, I'm barely writing my own thoughts. I feel like I archived a valuable part of me in an old, dusty drawer in the bedroom at my parents' house back in Lima, where I grew up. But what happened?

I guess I could blame it on my first seven years of work. Marketing, branding, ad campaigns, social media. It fried a few wires, and suddenly essays shrank into 140 characters and seven-word video hooks. No wonder I was burned out on my marketing job and my content creator phase.

Or I could make the *excuse* that I don't feel comfortable writing in English, which is completely true, by the way. I have no doubts my writing skills in Spanish are far superior, and my struggle to communicate in English is still real. That's a story worth an entry of its own.

Naturally, I have tried to get back into writing multiple times in the last three years, with no real success. The problem is now clear to me: I was writing with a goal and audience in mind. Not for the sake of writing, but with the Publish button at the end of the tunnel.

I've convinced myself that I don't care if anybody reads this. I just want to get back to it. To writing again.
